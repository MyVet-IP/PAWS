🥦 Nutrition

Balanced Diet for Your Pet

A proper diet is the foundation of your pet's health. Make sure to provide age-appropriate food with the right balance of proteins, carbohydrates, and fats.

✓
Choose food appropriate for your pet's life stage
✓
Avoid giving human food — many are toxic to pets
✓
Always keep fresh water available
✓
Measure portions to prevent obesity
🏃 Exercise

Keep Them Active & Happy

Regular physical activity prevents obesity, strengthens muscles, and improves your pet's mental wellbeing. Each species has different needs.

✓
Dogs need at least 30 min of exercise daily
✓
Cats benefit from interactive toys and climbing structures
✓
Avoid intense exercise in extreme heat
✓
Introduce new activities gradually
💉 Preventive Care

Vaccines & Deworming

Prevention is always better than cure. Keeping your pet's vaccination schedule up to date protects them and the whole family.

✓
Schedule annual vaccine boosters
✓
Deworm every 3 months for dogs and cats
✓
Use flea and tick prevention year-round
✓
Keep a health record for your pet
🦷 Dental Health

Don't Ignore Their Teeth

Dental disease affects over 80% of pets by age 3. Good oral hygiene prevents pain, infections, and even heart disease.

✓
Brush teeth 2–3 times per week
✓
Offer dental chews approved by vets
✓
Schedule annual dental cleanings
✓
Watch for bad breath — it's a warning sign
🧠 Mental Wellbeing

Emotional Health Matters Too

Pets experience stress, anxiety, and boredom. A stimulating environment and quality time with you are essential for their emotional balance.

✓
Provide enrichment toys and puzzles
✓
Maintain consistent daily routines
✓
Socialize puppies and kittens early
✓
Never punish — use positive reinforcement
✂️ Grooming

Grooming is Health Care

Regular grooming does more than keep your pet looking great. It helps you detect lumps, skin issues, or parasites before they become serious.

✓
Brush coat weekly to prevent matting
✓
Trim nails every 3–4 weeks
✓
Clean ears monthly to prevent infections
✓
Check skin for unusual lumps or redness
